Act I · Lessons
What Melers taught us
Goal The honest lessons — direct from Varia's proto-knowledge. These are what shape every deal that comes after.
  • Document workflows before closing. Founders don't know when they "task switch" — it's all a blur. Video every routine.
  • Accounting firm lock-in is real. They pick the software (even bad ones), and switching is painful. Negotiate this upfront.
  • Banks default to 5-year repayment — push hard for 7. It's on the table.
  • Operators need TES knowledge, management skills, and project management fluency — not just energy. Vet for this.
  • Build for anyone-can-leave from day 1. ConnectTeam, video SOPs, no single-point dependencies. Owner transition exposes every gap.
  • CRM doesn't integrate with accounting software cleanly. Budget the friction — or choose the stack together before closing.
  • DISCUSS: Which of these surprised you most? What's still not solved?
