Business Overview

Auran Pesupojat Oy is a single-operator cleaning and laundry service based in Turku, operating from a 270 m² workshop at Iso-Heikkiläntie 4 since 2015. The business offers textile cleaning, laundry services, and — a growing niche — boat cover cleaning and restoration. Revenue is approximately 80% consumer (B2C) and 20% commercial (B2B).

The company is owned and run entirely by Asko Haahtela, who has built a loyal customer base organically without any active marketing. The business has never needed to advertise; customers from as far as Inari travel to Turku specifically for the boat cover service, indicating genuine market scarcity in this segment.

Financials are clean: no debt, consistent profitability over four years, and cash on the balance sheet. The business is being sold via broker Suomen Yrityskaupat Oy. Asko is retiring and is willing to transition over up to 12 months at 4,000€/month, training incoming staff. He has no plans to set up a competing operation.

Equipment note: The premises are fitted with industrial laundry and cleaning equipment. Book value is 16K (near fully depreciated), but Asko indicates full replacement would cost upwards of 200K. The age and remaining useful life of the equipment is a critical DD item — see Risk Assessment.

Growth Upside

The base case uses conservative 5% annual revenue growth. Two specific opportunities could materially exceed this:

Boat cover cleaning

Currently >10% of revenue (~20K+) with zero marketing. Customers travel from across Finland because the service is genuinely scarce. Askel's digital marketing capability could convert this into a national brand — think direct online booking, SEO, social proof. Even capturing demand from three or four major coastal cities at current pricing would double this segment. This is the most interesting organic growth lever in the business.

Melers synergy

Askel's Melers industrial washing facility already generates 10K in confirmed synergy by internalising carpet and textile cleaning currently outsourced to third parties. This is in the base case. Additional cross-selling between Melers and Pesupojat clients is possible and not yet modelled.

Summary

Auran Pesupojat is a well-run, debt-free, cash-generating micro-business with ten years of operational history in Turku. It fits Askel's acquisition thesis — profitable, overlooked, and with a motivated seller willing to support a clean transition. The financials are transparent and the deal structure is straightforward.

At 190–195K (3.9x year-1 EBITDA), the entry price is fair. The return profile is strong: 24K net income to equity in year 1 on a 38K equity investment (63% cash-on-cash), loan fully repaid by 2032, and an optionality upside in the boat cover segment that is currently untapped.

The main risks are key-person concentration and equipment replacement capex. Key-person is partially mitigated by the structured 12-month transition, deferred payment, and confirmed retirement (no compete). The equipment risk is material — a 200K+ replacement cost against 49K annual EBITDA is a significant exposure that must be assessed before closing. If remaining equipment life exceeds 7–10 years, the deal stands on its own merits. If it is shorter, price or SPA terms need to reflect it.

Recommendation: authorise offer at 190K, ceiling 195K, conditional on (a) satisfactory equipment condition report and (b) completion of tax DD and lease confirmation.
