Case Study

Melers Oy

Commercial laundry services — Turku, Finland

Melers Oy is a profitable, two-location commercial laundry in Turku, operating since 1967 and under current ownership for over 30 years. It serves care homes, hotels, and households with stable margins.

Laundry is an essential, recession-resilient service. Melers has built long-term customer relationships through quality and consistency.

With operations largely manual, there is clear potential to introduce modern systems and improve efficiency without changing what customers value.

We acquired Melers in December 2025 as the first asset in our long-term portfolio.

01

Discovery

Sourced through our broker network and evaluated against our core criteria.

An essential service business with recurring B2B demand, strong margins, and a founder approaching retirement without a succession plan.

Most opportunities do not pass. This one did.

02

Due Diligence

We verified that the earnings were real.

Customer concentration, cost structure, equipment condition, staffing stability, and cash flow durability were reviewed in detail. Multiple site visits and direct discussions with the owners confirmed operational resilience.

We do not buy revenue. We buy cash flow.

03

Negotiation

Acquired at a sensible multiple relative to earnings.

The structure aligned incentives through a multi-year earn-out and preserved strong debt coverage from operating cash flow. Terms were designed to protect continuity for customers, staff, and the founding team.

04

Close

Less than 100 days from first contact to ownership transfer.

A structured handover ensured knowledge transfer, operational stability, and retention of key relationships.

05

Strengthening

Post-acquisition focus has been on introducing structure and modern systems.

Financial reporting has been standardised. Workflow and routing processes are being optimised. Digital tools are being implemented where they improve efficiency.

The business retains its name, local identity, and long-standing customer relationships.
